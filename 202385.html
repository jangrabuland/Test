What Is It

Main Product Information

Verizon One Talk is an advanced mobile and office business communications system that integrates smartphones, desk phones, computers and tablets into a unified solution.

Verizon One Talk is an advanced mobile and office business communications system that integrates smartphones, desk phones, computers and tablets into a unified solution, including:

  • Voice, video and messaging communications
  • Automated Receptionist
  • Hunt groups
  • Call queues
  • Advanced business and user features

OneTalk untethers businesses' primary business number from physical real estate and transforms it into a cloud-native asset.

  • This allows staff to move a live conversation from a desk phone to the mobile app with zero interruption.
  • The result is a system that:
    • Reduces customer friction
    • Eliminates missed opportunities
    • Provides consistent front-end experience across all locations and devices

One Talk allows users to easily manage business and user features from the Verizon One Talk web portal or mobile app.

  • One Talk includes multiple user device and app options:
    • Smartphone Native Dialer: Connect mobile employees to the office and customers from their smartphone native keypad all over the reliable and award-winning Verizon network.
    • Desk, Conference and Cordless Phones: Enhance calling and collaboration with a range of Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) phones including the nation's first 4G cellular enabled desk phone and accessories for office-based employees.
    • Mobile App for Smartphones and Tablets: Enable employees using business owned or personal smartphones and tablets (including other carrier devices) to access One Talk features from a single number.
    • Desktop App for Computers: Easily make and receive voice/video calls and text messages on a computer (e.g., PC or MAC), similar to using a smartphone.
  • One Talk has powerful features to enhance communications:
    • Anywhere Calling: Provides flexibility and a better calling experience with business-grade features like Caller ID and Simultaneous Ring.
    • Easy Collaboration: Boosts employees' ability to collaborate more effectively with messaging (e.g., chat, text messaging and IM).
    • Engaging Pseudo MDN Customer Experience: Connects callers to the people they want to contact with features like Auto Receptionist, Hunt Groups and more.

Line Sharing allows calls made to a specific phone number to be answered on any device associated with that line, providing flexibility and convenience for users.

How Verizon One Talk Line Sharing works:

FeatureDescription
Single Phone Number
  • With Line Sharing enabled, a single phone number is assigned to a specific line within the One Talk system.
  • The assigned single phone number serves as the primary point of contact for incoming calls.
Multiple Devices
  • Multiple devices, such as desk phones, mobile devices and mobile/desktop apps, can be associated with the same MDN.
  • Each device is configured to place and receive calls for the shared MDN.
  • Example: A user has One Talk number on their smartphone (Native Dialer) as well as an office IP Desk Phone, home office IP Desk phone, laptop computer (app) and another smartphone (app) - all incoming calls ring devices simultaneously, and user can use any device for outgoing calls.
Call Routing
  • When a call comes into the shared MDN, the One Talk system routes the call to all devices associated with that MDN simultaneously.
  • Call Routing ensures that the user has the opportunity to answer incoming calls, regardless of the user's location or the device users are using.
  • Example: A One Talk user or other employee can answer a call to any of the configured endpoint devices.
Call Management
  • Users can manage calls on any device associated with the shared line, including answering, transferring and placing calls.
  • Allows for seamless communication and collaboration among team members, regardless of member's preferred communication device.
Line sharing
  • Line sharing with up to 3 simultaneous incoming and outgoing calls from any configured device.
  • Example: The One Talk user can be on a call from a smartphone and up to 2 other employees can use 1 of the configured Desk Phones or apps to make and receive calls at the same time (on a smartphone, computer or tablet).

Endpoints

An endpoint refers to any device or location sharing the MDN where the One Talk service is used to make and receive calls.

  • Endpoints are optional additions to the primary device and are not required for all the One Talk features to function properly.
  • Virtual numbers (AR, HG and CQ) cannot have endpoints added to them, therefore Line Sharing does not apply to them.
  • The only devices capable of participating in Line Sharing are:
    • Smartphones
    • Desk Phones
    • Mobile/Computer apps (smartphones/tablets/PCs/laptops)

Note:

  • Line Sharing feature does not include Auto Attendant, Hunt Group or Call Queue numbers.
  • Apps are available for smartphones/tablets (iOS and Android) and computers (Mac and Windows).

Important: Understanding Data and Avoiding Overage Charges

It is important to inform the customer about the Backup Mobile Hotspot's data impact to avoid customer billing surprises.

Key Takeaway

The desk phone plus all the devices connected to its hotspot share a 5 GB allowance. Exceeding this limit incurs an extra $15 fee per gigabyte used.

Think of a customer's data usage with this simple formula: If the Total Monthly Usage is greater than the customer's plan allowance, the overage charge applies.

Example scenario:

  • The Customer's plan includes 5 GB of data per month.
  • A Customer's desk phone uses 1 GB for its own functions.
  • Customers connect their laptop to the hotspot and use 14 GB for videoconferences and work.
  • Customer's Total Monthly Usage would be 15GB.
  • In this case, the customer exceeded their allowance by 10 GB, so Verizon adds an overage charge for that amount to their bill.

Wireless Priority Service (WPS) Overview

WPS is a White House directed cellular communications service provided and managed by the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) in compliance with the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) (FCC 00-242).

  • WPS provides authorized devices with priority calling on all nationwide and several regional cellular networks.
    • WPS calls do not preempt calls in progress or deny the general public use of telephone networks.
  • WPS carriers allow eligible devices to have priority calling on the service provider's network when dialing the *272 service code.

What Is It

WPS is a basic wireless service enhancement that allows National Security / Emergency Preparedness (NS/EP) calls to have priority service.

  • WPS improves end-to-end call completion during emergencies.
  • WPS is an enhancement that helps support events that trigger tremendous telephone traffic (e.g., disasters, terrorist attacks and major accidents).

What Does It Do

WPS provides enhanced priority throughout the network for HD Voice calls only.

  • This allows for more successful connection rates than that of non-priority users.
  • WPS provides LTE Radio Access Network (RAN) priority on VoLTE without having to dial *272.
  • WPS customers receive priority throughout the network during times of congestion on the VoLTE network.

Who Can Get It

WPS requires Department of Homeland Security (DHS) approval.

  • Customers must follow the DHS approval process to receive WPS.
  • The Public Sector team adds feature codes to the customer's lines after the customer has been approved.
  • Customers are not conditioned by root/group, GID or CUST type.

Porting – How it Works in One Talk

When a customer wants to port a number to One Talk, it is often the most important number for that business.

Handle porting business numbers to One Talk with caution and ensure customers have their One Talk device ready to receive the ported number.

  • Porting a wireless mobile number to One Talk works like any other wireless port and takes about 2 hours to complete.
  • Verizon system handles wireline numbers differently.
    • Wireline ports trigger a specific porting flow that begins with a customer selecting the day they want the port to complete.
      • The company porting out the number, then confirms the selected date or provides a new date.
    • During the porting process for wireline ports:
      • The original device continues to receive inbound calls, while the new device can only place outbound calls.
      • Once the port completes, the new device can place and receive calls.
  • The controls in place that determine when the port completes:
    • Under normal circumstances, the port progresses through several steps and notifies the customers of the progress until they receive the port completion email.
    • If fallout occurs during the porting process, the:
      • Port Center manually completes the port.
      • Customer cannot receive any notifications until the port is complete.
  • Communications the customers receive during the porting process:
    • Wireless ports only receive a port completion email once the port completes.
    • Wireline ports receive 5 email notifications:
      • Confirmation of the selected date.
      • 5-day reminder.
      • 3-day reminder.
      • 1-day reminder.
      • Port completion.
  • For questions/issues, contact the port center for assistance: 888-844-7095.

Restrictions

  • Warning: Do not attempt to use the Port In Telephone Number Change (TNC) process on any line (port in number or the temporary number) with One Talk service.
  • Anytime the One Talk SFO is added during the port in TNC process on the line, it generates a system billing error (EA, ES, EN) on the account.
  • If the port TNC process is used and port in is incomplete, then wait for the successful completion before adding the One Talk SFO. Otherwise, it's best to use the direct port in process on any One Talk subscriber line.
  • If you use the direct port in process then the One Talk SFO can be included with the port request – in this scenario you do not have to wait for the port completion before adding the One Talk SFO.

Impact: Failing to abide by this policy causes the temporary number and port in number, unable to place or receive calls until rectified by the WCC.

Note: If your customer has the One Talk SFO on a temporary number, cancel the current temporary number and create a new order with a direct port in order finally, call WCC for further assistance.
